A Productive Wednesday

On Wednesday, I opened my wardrobe and got ready for an online webinar. Before it started, I organized some kitchen ware and thought better warehousing would save space. Luckily, my new laptop was still under warranty.

Outside, a wasp landed on the window, while a wingless insect crawled across the ground in the wintry weather. A cat quietly cleaned its whiskers.

The speaker from Wellesley encouraged us to stay zealous, whereas some students kept wonder whether success depends more on talent or persistence.

With regard to my project, a Windsor described an accident full of woe. His story became a useful yardstick for judging the evidence.

After a short workout, I finished my workbook, completed every worksheet, and attended a workshop. Although our ideas were worlds apart, working together was worthwhile.

Later, I wrung a wet towel because my wrist was sore. I also received a legal writ. The extra effort finally yielded good results, carrying more weight than I had expected. Someone even joked that I could whip any challenge.
